A LOOK BACK IN THE FUTURE

In 1968, Stanley Kubrick’s sci-fi epic 2001: A Space Odyssey pushed movies light years into the future. It pioneered the use of special effects, makeup, sound design…and needle drops.

“Kubrick would have made a good scientist; he insists on knowing how everything works, and grasps new ideas, however complex, almost instantly.”

ARTHUR C. CLARKE

Screenplay, Novel
STANLEY KUBRICK
Director, Producer, Screenplay

I didn't have to try for ambiguity; it was inevitable. And I think in a film like 2001, where each viewer brings his own emotions and perceptions to bear on the subject matter, a certain degree of ambiguity is valuable, because it allows the audience to "fill in" the visual experience themselves. In any case, once you're dealing on a nonverbal level, ambiguity is unavoidable.
